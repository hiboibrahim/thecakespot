FAQ

Planning your first trip to Turkey? We're here to answer all your questions and help you make the most of your journey.

While Turkey is generally a safe destination for travelers, it is always a good idea to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to protect your safety. Overall, by taking common-sense precautions and staying aware of your surroundings, you can help ensure a safe and enjoyable trip to Turkey.

The official language of Turkey is Turkish, which uses a different alphabet and grammar than many other languages. While many people in Turkey's major cities speak English, it is still a good idea to learn some basic Turkish phrases to help you communicate with locals and navigate your travels.

Yes, it is possible to work remotely in Turkey as a digital nomad. Turkey has a growing community of remote workers and is becoming an increasingly popular destination for digital nomads. However, there are a few things to consider before making the decision to work remotely in Turkey.

The cost of living in Turkey varies depending on the location and lifestyle. In general, the cost of living in Turkey is lower than in many other countries in Europe and North America. However, prices can vary widely depending on the region and city you are in. For example, the cost of living in Istanbul, Turkey's most crowded city, is higher than in other parts of the country.

Turkey is a popular destination for travelers all year round, but the busiest time is typically from May to September. During these months, tourist destinations can be particularly crowded. If you prefer a less crowded experience, consider visiting in April or October, which tend to be more popular among digital nomads.

As a result of the COVID-19 pandemic, Turkey has become a popular destination for digital nomads. Istanbul is particularly popular due to its lower cost of living and stunning beauty. In a survey of digital nomads, 90% said they prefer to live in coastal countries, and Turkey has some of the best coasts in the world. With its beautiful beaches and rich culture, it's no surprise that many nomads are choosing to call Turkey home.

The digital nomad movement is growing!
